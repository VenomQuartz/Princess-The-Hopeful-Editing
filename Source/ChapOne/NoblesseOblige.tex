\section*{The Light and the Dark}%(general cosmology)
\phantomsection
\label{sec:LightAndTheDark}

That's the thing about morality, it's complicated. Sages and philosophers have debated for thousands of years to answer the simplest of questions. \textquotedblleft What is good?\textquotedblright, \textquotedblleft What is evil?\textquotedblright\, But is morality always so complicated? Are there not easier questions to ask? For every tough question like \textquotedblleft Is it justified to kill one to save five?\textquotedblright\, is there not an easier question like \textquotedblleft Is it justified to kill another for your own personal profit?\textquotedblright

Ask any of the Nobility and they will tell you that of course there is such a thing as objective good and evil. To the Radiant the world they inhabit makes this as clear as daylight. A Princess draws her magic from the Light. A force created by love, friendship, by effort to improve the world or just effort to help a stranger out. The Light probably isn't the absolute axis for all that is good but if it could think you could say it's making a sincere effort to reach that exalted height. For the Nobility this is enough to point at for proof that good really does exist, because what Princesses compare the Light to is the Dark.

The Dark is the Light's opposite in every way. It is a force of banal corruption; the absence of all hope, the absence of all belief that things can get better. It festers where people commit acts of cruelty and inflict suffering on each other. Everything it touches is tainted with its own nature. If left unchecked it would cause an unending torrent of corruption until the Dark finally succumbed to its own nature and starved upon an empty wasteland. The scholars among the Nobility are divided upon one key question about the Dark. Its origins. Some say that it is a dreadful alien being attacking our world through humanity's cruelties for it's own sustenance. Others say that we ourselves are the Dark's true origin, our sins created it and we shall forever be haunted by our crimes until such a day as we rise above them. 

The contrast between Light and Dark is something every Noble is glad for. For as much as she looks to the Light, even the Nobility live somewhere between Light and Dark. Sooner or later every Princess will have to make some tough moral choices; it comes with the job. On a bad day a Princess could find herself trapped for an unconscionable time with no clear answers. Struggling to stitch together some semblance of a moral solution to an impossible problem. On those days the existence of the Light can be the only thing reminding them that good exists and it is worth fighting for. 

\subsection*{The Kingdom's Fall}%(what is known of the Kingdom and its fall to the Darkness)

When the Nobility speak of the Kingdoms they refer to at least eight sovereign nations: Alhambra, Andarta, Aztallan, The Confederacy of Four Winds, Crystal Heril, Danann, Gonel and Wen-Mung. There are others, but their names, histories and even their existence are debated with little consensus. Each lived the ideals of the Light in their own way, guided by the Queen who ruled them. None were perfect but by all accounts they were fantastic places to live. Prosperous, just, kind and peaceful. 

Yet for all its Light the Kingdoms did not last forever. Even in a world of Light some things are universal, one is the necessity of compromise. A land of individual freedom must accept a lack of organisation as its due. Each of the Queens chose what they deemed to be good and accepted the cost. In time they forgot that this was a choice of preference and believed they alone had chosen the one true way. In their own way each turned rigid and dogmatic; from this simple vice the Darkness gained its first foothold in its eternal war against the Kingdom. 

The Kingdoms rallied and drove the Darkness back to whatever terrible domain it came from as they had done countless times before but this time they were blinded by their dogma. Small traces of Taint were overlooked, festering as \textquotedblleft inevitable drawbacks\textquotedblright\, of the Light's civilisation. Rotting away in secret the Darkness began to grow within the heart of the Kingdom. Its attacks became more frequent, more destructive, and every time it was defeated the same growing rot remained unnoticed. In the end the Darkness broke over the Kingdom like a tidal wave, the armies were routed and the cities fell, still trying to understand how the Darkness' presence was even possible. 

Today little remains of the Kingdom. The domains of the five Radiant Queens live on in the Dreamlands, but the warping Gales make it hard to tell how accurate a portrayal those dreams are. If any true records of the Kingdom exist they would be locked away deep within Alhambra's vaults, for of the Kingdom only Alhambra has survived to this day. Protected by it's Queen's formidable magic deep within the Heart of the Darkness. 

\subsection*{The Long Night and the Dream}%(Princesses in the World of Darkness before the Release)

The Darkness cannot create, it can only corrupt. This was always its greatest weakness. Among those who were Tainted by the Darkness during the Fall were three mighty creatures known as The Red Queen, The White Queen and The Black Queen: The avatars of violence, self loathing and despair. These creatures knew the Light intimately, they knew that even as the Darkness turned upon itself the Light would ready itself to build something new and wonderful to reclaim its place.

Guided by the Darkness' instinctive opposition to the Light the three constructed a trap deep within the Dreamlands: A mirror to reflect the Light of the world. The trap tricked the souls of the Nobility travelling towards reincarnation and drew them away from Earth. They incarnated within the Dreamlands where wonderful and elaborate illusions clouded their minds; letting them believe they lived and did their duties on Earth even while the centuries ticked away.

The Radiant call this period of history \textquotedblleft The Long Night\textquotedblright and the trap that held them is simply called \textquotedblleft The Dream\textquotedblright. For much of human history the Radiant hardly had any presence at all. Reincarnations have always formed the vast majority of the Nobility and with the reincarnations locked away anyone who Blossomed was most likely alone and untrained. If they were lucky a group of friends might Blossom together. They would do what they can, but standing alone there was little chance of changing the world.

Only the Twilight Queens created any form of continuity for the Nobility during the Long Night. The Lady of Alhambra kept the last remnants of the Kingdoms alive. The Saraphic General inducted mortal forces to throw against the Darkness. The Queen of Mirrors searched for her True Heir. The Twilight Queens were a temptation to the Princesses who Blossomed during the Long Night, but they were a temptation because they were so vital. The only constant source of support a young Princess could turn to. 

\subsection*{The War of Hope}%(The Release, the first decades of the Princesses' return, the present)

In July 1969 mankind first set foot on the moon. The hope released by this single event was so great that for the briefest instant the Light shone brighter on Earth than it did in the Dreamlands. In a flash every soul trapped within knew that they were not on Earth as they believed. The five Queens mustered their armies against their jailors and shattered the machinery of their prison. Their victory was swift and brutal; after millennia of imprisonment the Kingdom was once again free. 

The effects were far from instantaneous. The first reincarnated Princess to once again Blossom was in 1987 and the second was not until 1994. Today the Nobility are still small in number but they're growing fast with multiple Blossomings a year. At this point they are a minor force in the world but have had some noticeable successes in areas where Princesses are common; Blossomings have shown a tendency towards geographical clustering. The first generation of Princesses have now reached middle age and many have gained positions of prestige and influence. In doing so they have caused a backlash from established power blocks, both mortal and otherwise. The Twilight Queens have also experienced a renaissance; each was active through the Long Night and were perfectly placed to take advantage of the release. New and vulnerable Princesses have been eagerly snapped up by all three Courts. 

As the saying goes, we live in interesting times.

\subsubsection*{Alternative Dates}
As 1969 recedes further into the past the implications of moon landing triggering the release change: The average age of Princesses increases. Princesses have more time to improve the world, and questions of what they have or have not accomplished become more pressing. One solution to these questions is to choose a later date for The Release, or perhaps a much earlier date to facilitate a game set in a historical era without inflicting the problems of the Long Night upon your players. Some possible alternative triggers for the release are: \\

\textbullet The enlightenment of Gautama Buddha; 528 or 445 BCE \textemdash\, The Light meets the original enlightened. Princess' focus on community and the world contrasts with Buddha's philosophy of detachment. What shall they learn from each other? 

\textbullet The end of the Black Death, 1350 \textemdash\, Though a time of terror and suffering even the Black Death had it's silver lining, such as increasing social mobility and freeing peasants from serfdom. Could Princesses have helped usher in these changes? 

\textbullet The Reformation; 1517 \textemdash\, A time of religious dogma enforced by the Inquisition, Machiavellian and mercantile intrigue, a time crying out for reform and the Light.

\textbullet The ascension of Tokugawa Ieyasu; 1603  \textemdash\, Tokugawa's coronation marked the end of a brutal civil war and the start of the Edo period. A time of peace, prosperity, strict social order, and flourishing arts. It is the period that first comes to mind upon hearing the words \textquotedblleft feudal Japan\textquotedblright . Did Nobles play their part in creating this peace and prosperity?

\textbullet The 13th amendment; 1865 \textemdash\, The abolition of slavery, but the beginning of a long struggle for equality. A struggle worthy of any Princess' dedication. 

\textbullet The Battle of Shiroyama; 1877 \textemdash\, The Meiji Restoration was A  time of growth and renewal as Japan reconsolidated and prepared to renter the outside world. \textbullet VE day; 1945 \textemdash\, The end of World War II would release Princesses just in time for Nobles to come of age in the 60s, a time of massive social change, clashing cultures and counter-cultures, and the Cold War.

\subsection*{Blossoming}

A Princess may be born as the reincarnation of her past self with a dormant seed of magic in her heart, or she may be a mortal who's purity and passion has such strength that it will erupt into a Light so strong it will shine through every life she lives, but a Princess is always born as a seemingly ordinary mortal. She lives, grows, and learns in the safety of her parents home until she is ready to step forth into the darkness and face the evil therein. 

Nobles call the transition between mundanity and Nobility a Blossoming. The moment a child achieves her potential and first becomes the woman she will be. It is an unmistakeable and turbulent yet joyous time in any Princess' life. Facing a problem that seems beyond her, and beyond the adults or authorities who shelter her, the Princess-to-be declares or discovers that no, she is able to solve this and she decrees that with her capability comes the responsibility to act. Princesses describe this as self affirming, the realization of their agency and the actualization of their ideals and self-development in one critical moment. Many report a feeling that they could \textquotedblleft never forgive themselves\textquotedblright\, or \textquotedblleft would no longer be me\textquotedblright\, if they had acted any other way. If the Princess is faced with a time critical emergency she might Transform on the spot, striding forward to meet the challenge with new magic. Others Blossom over hours, days, or even weeks, as the Princess works towards a solution or explores her new-found sense of self in the aftermath of her victory. As she progresses towards her Blossoming the world opens up before her, she finds answers to questions and doubts that had plagued her, and feels ever more certain of her place in it until she accumulates a critical mass of positive emotions that condense into her Noble magic.

Because Blossomings begin when a Princess realises that she is capable and responsible for solving real issues the majority of Blossoming occur from the mid teens to early adulthood as the individual establishes their own social identity and faces new challenges. Younger Princesses are usually sheltered from the kind of problems that prompt a Blossoming, but circumstances push some to accept responsibility beyond their years and anything from taking care of a younger sibling while mother works to running away from home can result in a young Princess claiming the magic she needs to overcome whatever troubles she faces. Not all of these child Princesses have a specific trigger to their Blossoming. Some reincarnated Nobles describe their Blossoming as a natural product of a growing awareness of themselves and their role as their brains developed and they explored the world around them. As though they already were a Princess and only needed to realise it. Blossomings in adulthood are relatively rare because so many Princesses have already Blossomed before they settle down into their adult life, and such Blossomings normally occur when a major life event such as falling in love, an encounter with the supernatural, or becoming pregnant forces the Princess to re-evaluate herself and her place in the world. These Blossomings can be harder on the Princess as an older Princess has more responsibilities and less time for magical quests. Alhambran tales claim that the girl who would become the Queen of Tears Blossomed at her birth, and the delay in Blossomings today is merely a sign of the corruption of the world; but most Radiant Nobles dismiss this as the self-aggrandisement common to the Court of Tears and choose to believe that it has always been this way.

\subsection*{Callings}

A Princess Blossoms when she is faced with a crisis and chooses to act, she accepts responsibility for the problems surrounding her and declares it is her duty to fix things. Through accepting duty and responsibility the Princess finds the inner strength she'll need to fulfil her new role, and unlocks her magic. Although Blossoming (usually) has no effect on a Princess' brain development or hormonal balance many Nobles consider their Blossoming to signify the moment they became adults: Ready to handle their own responsibilities, make their own decisions to govern their life, and go out into the world to make their mark. 

This combination of a Princess' new duties, and the strength and magic that flow from them is called her Calling; the foundation stone of any Princess' power. Through her Calling a Princess can recharge her spent magical and emotional energy: By fulfilling the responsibilities she has accepted she refreshes her spirit and her surety about her place in the world. A Calling also brings a Princess affinity for the magic that is most helpful to fulfilling her duties, making it easier for her to learn those abilities. Finally a Calling also imposes a code of behaviour, for a Princess has magic to help her fulfil her purpose and make the world a better place, not to indulge herself in the privileges of noble rank.

A Calling does not confine a Princess into a narrow role, and few Princesses would argue that it should. The world is chaotic and always has more problems than Princesses so the ability to step outside your comfort zone to deal with a different kind of crisis is always praiseworthy; while accepting a Calling makes it easier for a Princess to learn certain types of magic, it does nothing to hinder her exploration into other fields. 

Perhaps most importantly of all a Calling is an understanding and an acceptance that a Princess' duties are not a job she performs or a role she fills. A Princess' duties are who she is, and who she wants to be. 

\subsection*{Common Lives}

For most hours of the day a Princess exists as her mundane identity, with little in the way of magical power, and for most Princess their activities during this time are as mundane as their bodies. They have work for school or college, friends to see, and family to have dinner with. As a rule Nobles are social creatures, the kind of person who'd cut contact with their friends and family to dedicate themselves to hunting monsters twenty four hours a day is less likely to Blossom than one who'd want to balance their friends and family alongside their new duties. What's more, the good times and pleasant emotions she experiences eating a simple family meal are the fuel for Noble magic. A competent princess cherishes and invests time into the friendships that give her strength. 

The majority of Princesses are in their teenage years and frequently (though, sadly, not always) have their parents as passive support, but must suffer problems with their task and how it interacts with their youth and the demands that society places upon them. Not too many people are pleased with their teenage children sneaking out, and are even less pleased when they find out that the child has been going down to the area of town where the drug dealers hang out, especially on a night when there are later reports of gang violence. Even Princesses whose duties do not involve violence still face scrutiny, for in the World of Darkness the supernatural is known to be dangerous. A Princess' protests that she's just working as a hospital volunteer that happens to have healing magic often fall on deaf ears. That said a lucky few Princesses have parents who're willing and able to help them keep their secret and provide assistance or act as the intermediary between their daughter and the adult world. 

But most Nobles keep their second life hidden, other Princesses can help, and many Nobles have a system of sleepovers or after school clubs where the Nobles who don't have any duties today cover for the ones who do. Magic is of course used too, and many Princesses use the intelligence or maturity of their Transformed self to get through homework quickly. It's not unknown for Nobles to impersonate each other with magical disguises or weave illusions to hide their absence from family. Some Princesses even hide their Transformed identity behind a screen when texting their friends or boyfriend, as the empathy and social acumen she acquires through Transformation can help smooth the hurt from a hastily cancelled date. 

At a slightly older age, there are the young adults, typically at university or just entering the workplace. In some ways, they are luckier, because they do not have to face the fact that they’re not legally adults; on the other hand, many a Princess has lost her job or been forced to drop out of university due to the stresses and demands of her calling, and unlike younger ones, their social safety net of family is in another city which means that the issue of rent and bills starts to come up. For every problem a young adult avoids through their increased freedom they must face another one due to their increased responsibilities. However young adulthood is a time of transition, and a disruption at this point in a Princess' life is often easier than at other times. Drifting away from friends you've met recently in university hurts far less than a Calling forcing a wedge between a Princess' and her parents. This is the age Nobles where Nobles find it easiest to transition into a new social circle that is compatible with their duties. Either because it is full of people in the know, or just full of people who're comfortable with hectic schedules and share the Princess' passion and Beliefs. 

Nobles who Blossomed as adults can find it the hardest of all, even before they Blossom a Princess tends to be outgoing and socially skilled, meaning an adult Princess is likely to have responsibilities, and perhaps even a family of their own. While teenagers can have strict boundaries set by their parents, such boundaries often allow regular sleep overs or nights out with friends. Sleeping regularly at a friends house is a whole different question when you're married with young kids. A teenager's funds are more modest than an adult, but they can easily transfer money spent on video games and socialising to fulfilling one's Calling. Money tied up in school fees and a mortgage is not so easily moved. Nobles who Blossomed younger then grew up into adulthood often build their adult lives around their dual identities and make full use of the freedom and flexibility adulthood provides, but those who Blossomed late are often too tied down to start a life of adventure; and instead balance their Calling around their mundane life. The pain of knowing you can help, and knowing it's your responsibility to do so, but being too tied down can be devastating to these Princesses. Most think very hard about simply telling their family. 

Pre-teen Princesses can find it just as hard as adults. Many live in families who expect that they, or a known trusted adult, will know where they are at all times. Even other Princesses can oppose their activities, teenage Princesses are often as uncomfortable with a six year old fighting monsters as anyone else (for their part The Radiant Queens have seen enough young Nobles to easily recognise who'll sneak out to fight against a Queen's advice and teach them how to survive rather than waste their breath telling them to stay home). One thing that must be taken into account is that young Blossomings (usually) don't happen without a reason, so more child Princesses than you might expect have a parents who need their kid's help and are forced by circumstances to be open minded, this can grow into a partnership. With supportive parents and placement with a new peer group that matches her quickly growing maturity there's no reason a pre-teen can't thrive. But when the Princess doesn't want her parents to know, or they know but insist their child has a normal childhood a young Princess can be well and truly trapped.

No matter what age she is in balancing work and friendship will require skill, and quite possibly magic. While Princesses can get lucky or build a life designed to be compatible with their second career, most Princesses find that there can be as much drama and difficulty in their mundane life as there is in their magical life, if perhaps not as much danger. While many Princesses wish to change society so the next generation will be free from the problems they face, few begrudge the time and effort it takes to keep their mundane life and their mundane friendships. If they were the kind of person who did they probably would never have Blossomed in the first place. 

\subsection*{Emotions are Magic}

The first bloom of magic in a Noble is joyous, but soon she realizes the price she must pay for it. A Princess' power comes not from some exotic energy that only she can see, her powers come from her emotions. Just as a mother, upon seeing her child trapped under a car, suddenly gains the strength she needs to lift that car; a Princess can draw upon her emotions to find the strength she needs to conjure fire, to heal wounds with a wave of a magic wand, or to fly. 

But just as a Princess' emotions can go forth and affect the world in ways unimaginable to mere mortals; her magic, her very nature, means that the world can always come into her heart and affect her emotions. Imagine a grown woman who's heart still flutters at every kiss as though it was her very first. Imagine a life time of fighting evil or practising medicine when every life lost hurts like the very first. And in the world of darkness for every kiss that burns with the passion of the first there are a dozen hurts that strike her exposed heart. This is the price a Princess pays. She can never form the armour around her heart that comes so easily to mortals, her own magic rips such protection open as it travels outwards. All she can do is grow strong enough to bear the weight and grow mature enough to deal with her emotions constructively. 

As a Princess grows in power and magic her emotions grow even stronger. This Sensitivity can quite literally make life unbearable. Simply walking among people batters her with every hardship she sees, each and every one feels like her personal responsibility. When she goes home the faces of everyone she couldn't help lie like Shadows over her thoughts. They flash before her eyes as she lies in bed, pushing her to go back out and fix things. Too many Princesses get trapped in a downward cycle, inaction or failure to help form Shadows upon their mind, leading to more failures and more Shadows, until she collapses in exhaustion and despair. 

But just as her emotions are stronger than any mortals, strong enough to summon storms and raise palaces from the ground, a Princess can also be stronger than any mortal. Every princess has ironclad Beliefs. A set of ideals that she knows, deep within her soul, that if she follows they will lead her to a better future no matter how dark today seems. Even when a Princess' is suffering emotional pain a regular mortal couldn't conceive of, let alone bear, her Belief can give her the strength to get out there and fight to fix whatever is upsetting her. The Sensitivity that opens her heart to the suffering of the world also opens her heart to the love of friends and family, and by spending time in their company and reconnecting with what is most important a Princess restores her will and moves on from past pain. For every face that looks to her in pain and need, another looks to her in gratitude for the aid she has given, lifting the burdens on her soul and allowing her to start fresh. Rested and energised for the fight ahead. 

The cycle of Sensitivity and Shadows is easiest to stop at the beginning, but with support and strength of character it can be broken at any time. 

And as for those who cannot bear the weight, or can't break the cycle. Their only choice is to run and hide. Stick to the quiet hours, find a nice walled palace or quiet village somewhere away from all the pain and cruelty of the world; but anyone who could easily abandon the world is unlikely to Blossom. 

\subsection*{Transformation}

From the moment she Blossoms every Princess has the ability to Transform. She is gifted with a phylactery. An icon of her nobility and a permanent reminder of her dreams and aspirations. With the simplest effort she takes those dreams and aspirations and adorns herself in her nobility. They flow through her body, transforming her into the woman she always wanted to be.  

The Nobility often tell each other that Princesses don't do magic, they are magical and Transformation is the proof. In her mundane identity a Princess is barely more magical than the average man, but in her transformed identity... Ah, what a difference that is. Magic flows through her body; it is on the level of blood or bone. Her magic makes her stronger, more charismatic, or smarter. Under magical examination a Princess can see that spells and enchantments are as much a part of her body as carbon atoms or neurotransmitters. 

Even her personality is different, but usually not by much. Part of it is the just the natural result of her magic: She is more confident because she is more capable, she makes more witty remarks because she can think of them faster. Both of her two selves are fundamentally her. In her mundane self the Princess is a product of her innate nature and her life experiences. In her transformed self she is also the product of her innate nature and her life experiences, but she adds and a smoothed out average of her life experiences from all her past lives and her dreams and aspirations into the mix to create her idealised self. 

The resulting Transformed Identity is still recognisable as the same person, or it would be if Princess' didn't have an innate magical defence to protect their secret identity. The personality traits she shares with the average of her past laves are emphasised and the traits which conflict are de-emphasised, the result is a Transformed self who resembles the mundane self in a specific situation. The Transformed identity of a Princess who spent her past lives as diplomats and courtiers might act like her mundane identity would act in a formal situation such as job interview; in addition to minor differences like knowing the correct way to formally address the Emperor of Japan. 

It's when a Princess' current life is radically different from her past lives that a strong personality difference emerges. Because a Princess has the same innate nature in every life this usually requires a strong external factor to shape her life experiences. For example, a Princess' who's curious innate nature led her to many lives as scholars and investigators, but who spent this life growing up in a fanatical cult that crushed independent thought, will discover that she Transforms into the independent free thinker she usually is. Such Princesses can experience an identity crisis but, with time and support, they usually bring there mundane self closer to their Transformed self. After all, her Transformed self incorporates her beliefs and ideals, no matter how hard the world has tried to beat them out of her, and the more lives a Princess has experienced the closer the average of those lives comes to harmony with her innate nature. A Princess' Transformed self can be a guide, helping her become the best and truest version of herself. Some Princesses need that more than others. 

Some Princesses experience personality changes from having a different biology in their two identities. \section*{Noble Culture}

\label{sec:NobleCulture}

A Princess was never meant to exist in isolation. Her strength comes from the friends she makes and the grateful smiles of the people she protects. In the Long Night Princesses Blossomed so rarely that most would never meet another of their kind, and most suffered struggled dearly to fulfil their Calling alone or turned to the only easily available support on offer: The Twilight Queens. \subsection*{D\'ebutante} 

Princesses who live near to each other rarely remain strangers for long. A new Princess is often eager to meet peers who can help her master her new magical abilities and provide support when dealing with the new dangers she faces from both within and without. While tracking down a Princess' mundane identity is near universally considered to be horrific behaviour beneath all but the Court of Tears, no such taboo exists against making contact with a stranger's Transformed identity (which is the easier one to find anyway). A large community of Princesses creates a wealth of rumours and urban legends to investigate, meaning that a freshly Blossomed Princesses unaware of her peers but eager to find some has a place to start. It is even easier for an experienced Princess to find a newbie. In cities where there is an established Princess population there's usually more than enough people plugged into the social grapevine (or prone to prophetic dreams) to notice the waves a new Princess makes, and there's plenty of magical tricks that can be applied to finding another Princesses. A smaller town may have far fewer Princesses, but news travels far faster, so it usually balances out.  

Even if there are no other local Princesses the social waves made by the Nobility often end up online, like most information in the modern world. A freshly Blossomed Princesses who's curious to know if there are others like her can search the internet for the gossip in the nearest city and then plan a trip to visit for a couple of days. These early investigations can be dangerous for the young Noble is away from familiar environments and many dangers lurk in the shadows of the World of Darkness; what's more rumours about Princesses often place them near whatever threat they're trying to deal with. However most people raised in the World of Darkness are raised to be cautious and so a young Princess will take care when looking for peers. 

All this presumes the responsibility for introducing new Nobles to the local community falls upon that community or the new girl. While this is increasingly the case as Nobles get more established and better at finding each other, responsibility for finding each other has never fell upon Princesses alone. Shortly after the Release the Queens gathered officials from their courts and bid them to follow the souls of Princesses to earth. These officials became the first Shikigami. Their spirits took possession of animals and set out following dreams in search of newly reincarnated Princesses to mentor, and are quick to introduce their protag\'e to the Queens and their courts. 

\subsection*{Cliques}

Once a new Princess has been introduced to the community, it's only natural for her to start making friends; as a rule Princesses are sociable and agreeable. And so, in a city with a large Princess population friendship Cliques will inevitably form. 

Cliques are just what the name implies, friendship groups. They go shopping, see movies, plait each other's hair or compete in fun magic contests. There is no obligation that a Clique will work together as a team to fulfil their duties. A Grace may find that of the local Princesses she has the most in common with a Champion and a Troubadour, they share tastes in books, balls, and boys, so even though they might disagree on politics, and their contrasting Callings guarantees they'll have different opinions on the most effective way they can help the city they still seek each other out when there is a new film to watch, a formal dance to attend, or when they need emotional support. But when it's time to work the Champion patrols the night while the Grace goes campaigning with people who share her Beliefs and politics, be they Nobles or mortals.  

Many Cliques go out of their way to avoid \textquotedblleft talking shop\textquotedblright . The primary purpose of a Clique is to help Princesses de-stress and recharge after the pressures of the job by providing friends who understand what it means to be a Princess and know when to (and have the power to) force a Princess to get a good nights sleep \textendash\, no ands, ifs or buts. If the two Graces in a Clique are supporting rival political parties then getting into political debates can ruin what should be a relaxing night (or it can be fun and invigorating). While Nobles usually have the social skills to avoid getting into an unwanted argument, part of those skills involve knowing when a Clique should have a rule against talking shop.

However when Princesses are reacting to a problem: Be it the Darkness, the Twilight Queens or something mundane like an earthquake, and a Princess' usual co-workers aren't sufficient a Clique is usually her first point of call for extra backup. Members of a Clique trust each other, know each other well enough to work well together, and of course they all have each other's phone numbers. 

\subsection*{Nakama}

Not all Princesses have the option or the desire to distinguish between their friends and their co-workers. For these Princesses a Nakama serves as an alternative to a Clique. The word \textquotedblleft Nakama\textquotedblright\, means a group of friends; translated into English you might say \textquotedblleft buddies\textquotedblright\, or \textquotedblleft gang\textquotedblright\, in the playful kids' use of the word. Among the Nobility (if they aren't Japanese) Nakama has a more restricted sense: a small group of Nobles who joined together or were thrown together (perhaps all Blossoming in the same town, or facing the same great crisis) and have become something very close to a family because of it. They share in each other's duties, lending the skills of their Callings and Courts to Nobles of different backgrounds or learning enough of another Nobles work to participate. They have compatible Beliefs, or have already negotiated a mutually beneficial compromise between their various Beliefs. Many Nakama go so far as to share the secret of their mundane identity with each other, especially if they wish to live together to save on rent or pool their magic into creating a magically defended home. 

While more city Princesses are in Cliques than Nakama in small towns and rural areas the reverse is true. If there's only one Champion in town, and she's too new to have found or trained reliable mortal monster hunters to work with, the only backup she can call upon on is the other local Princesses. A Mender is perfectly capable of learning the same battle magic as a Champion if she puts her mind to it, and her regular healing magic can remove wounds in the middle of a fight. While a lot of Princesses swear by the importance of having a space and some Noble friends who are separated from their work life, having backup when facing down monsters or when a political rival is spreading lies about you is even more important. So when needs must, Princesses can and will negotiate through their conflicting Beliefs to work together. 

A Nakama can include the actual families of the Nobles, as well as their Nakama in the Japanese sense - people with no trace of magic in them, but who are intimate parts of the Nobles' mundane lives. This is especially common if the Nakama was formed in response to a common threat, as Princesses are very defensive of their loved ones and if the only way to convince someone that monsters are real and they're infesting this town is to reveal your secret; then you may as well go a bit further and bring them into the Nakama's communal home where there's usually a Princess around to keep an eye out for danger and magical defences. 

\subsection*{Co-Workers}

Princesses may be driven by strong and often conflicting Beliefs but that never stopped humans from organising to further their goals, and the Nobility can be far more empathetic and persuasive than mortals. When Princesses want to find collaborators without committing to the all-encompassing intimacy of a Nakama they usually seek out like minded Princesses with compatible Beliefs. The constant hum of online forums, socialising in the Dreamlands, and local Princess hangouts mean that Princesses searching for like-minded peers have no shortage of places to look.

But Princesses are not limited to seeking out fellow Nobles. While a Princess' virtuous nature and magical power makes her an ideal co-worker no one should discount the abilities of ordinary mortals. In fact, many Radiant consider the idea of ignoring the possibility of working side by side with mortals and working only with fellow Nobles, perhaps with mortal subordinates, is a sign that a Princess is sliding down the slippery slope towards the vanity of The Court of Mirrors. Rather than seek out a group of Princesses to found an organisation dedicated to a specific cause most Princesses skip re-inventing wheel by joining a mortal institution that already works in the area. Perhaps she'll meet like minded Nobles there? 

Even Champions who wish to pit their mettle against the Outer Darkness and it's monsters can find old boy's networks of police clubs who trade information and jade knives, hunting clubs with a higher purpose than sport, and all manner of mortal societies dedicated to protecting mankind from supernatural predation (admittedly as a supernatural being herself a Princess might not be welcomed with open arms, so Champions are more likely that other Callings to build their own organisations from scratch or work exclusively with Nobles and Sworn). 

As a result Princesses can be found at all levels of society, though the number of public figures rumoured to be a Noble's mundane identity is far greater than the number of Princesses who actually are in high office. Menders are more likely to be found working in hospitals and clinics than in some enchanted shrine hidden in the inner city and publicised through rumour and word of mouth. The friends she makes among the doctors and nurses might be in on the secret and help deflect rumours of miraculous recoveries, or they might just gladly take the regular patients to free their \textquotedblleft genius but entirely mundane\textquotedblright\, colleague to work on the hardest cases. Graces manage corporations and defend vulnerable citizens pro bono in court rooms, the lawyers around her assist in her cases and are inspired to go the extra mile by her belief in justice. Champions serve as police officers and when she sees a nest of Darkspawn she knows exactly which officers will face it alongside her. After all, Princesses consider themselves to be human, it's their society too. 

Of course given the demographics of the noble community a fair number are using magic to appear older than they are or working with organisations who accept younger volunteers. 

\subsection*{The Queens and their Courts}

Every Princess knows that to make the world better she must improve herself, she must rise to the challenge. And Princesses knows that if she works hard enough and holds faith in herself she can become so much better, for the Nobility are guided by the Queens. The greatest champions of the Light, and a living testament to the heights any Princess might one day reach. 

It is not known exactly how many Queens (or Kings) once ruled the Kingdoms, of this unknown number only eight Queens still have the power to affect the world. Of this eight: Five are dead, one is living, another is presumed to be living, and one is... Transformed. Collectively the five dead Queens are known as the Radiant, for they remain loyal to the light in spirit as well as word, for centuries the Radiant Queens were trapped deep within the Dreamlands, but now their trap has fallen. From their Palaces within the Dreamlands they call out to Princesses, seeking Hopeful agents who will restore the Light upon Earth. 

The Queens are the leaders and linchpins of Hopeful society in more ways than one. Their courts and kingdoms in the dreamlands can be reached by any Princess from the comfort of her bed, and Princesses frequent them looking for places where they can help, Nakama's to join, tutors to train them in their new magical abilities, or just where to find new friends in their local area. On a personal level a Queen is a living (or otherwise, these days) font of magic. Her Beliefs have become a coloured mirror that shines additional Light onto the world; everyone who shares her ideals can draw upon the Queen's Invocation to gain greater power or unlock new abilities that they would not otherwise have been possible. So while a Princess may disagree with a Queen's philosophy she is sure to be familiar with it and have given it due consideration. 

The Court's philosophies tend towards general applicability. The Queen of Diamonds, who emphasises clear and careful thought, is more interested in teaching Princesses how to think than what to think. The Queen of Hearts emphasises the role of communities and traditions while supporting her courtiers in following their traditions, not her own. Partly because each remembers what happened the last time they became dogmatic, but mostly this is because the Queens must now speak to a wider audience than a single nation state built around a shared culture. As a result Princesses as far flung as Tokyo and Timbuktu have taken the same core philosophies and adapted it to their needs, while retaining enough of a common reference to enable communication and collaboration. 

While no Queen is capable of using her full power on Earth. The five Radiant Queens are dead, the Queen of Tears will not leave Alhambra, the Queen of Storms will not abandon her battle in the Dark World, and while nobody knows what is up with the Queen of Mirrors it is generally accepted that she doesn't act directly. The Queens can still provide advice and guidance. They are people who have built and won the hearts of nations. As monarchs and rulers they were perhaps the very best in all of history; their Kingdoms may have fallen but all kingdoms fall in time, few were utopian at their apex. As mentors and teachers the Radiant Queens have more to often than a young Princess could imagine.  

\subsection*{The Wider Community}

Like the modern mortal societies they serve, much of a Princess' social life happens online. Once you go beyond the close friendships of a Princess' Clique or Nakama, and Nobles or mortals who work alongside her in the day job, the Princess' community continues on the internet. 

In the short time since the Release a sprawling culture has grown across a network of blogs, forums and social networking sites. The Hopeful community covers more than just Princesses; Beacons and Sworn are welcome and even ordinary people can often be found on the comment threads. The online Radiant community is hardly a walled garden; it spills forth onto related subjects. Princesses can often be found blogging about and arguing in forums related to art, politics, current affairs, activism and social justice. Many blogs or forums are considered to be both hubs of the Radiant community \textendash\, for the author or prominent members are Princesses \textendash\, and considered to be ordinary websites by their mundane viewers \textendash\, for the Princesses are still in the closet (at least under this username). 

Inevitably debates between so many young, passionate ideologues often becomes full of intractable arguments. Since Princesses are almost invariably socially competent these arguments are, if not productive, at least good at getting Princesses to think from multiple points of view. People often wonder how Princesses, a group who live by the maxim \textquotedblleft School, friends, royal duties, sleep, choose three\textquotedblright\, ever find the time to argue on the internet. In truth it's common for Princesses to vanish entirely from their online community when an actual problem comes to their town; lives have even been saved because somebody decided check up why a someone stopped posting. 

When compared to the Dreamlands the internet has two advantages. Firstly it's more convenient, meeting another Princess in the Dreamlands requires luck or a pre-arranging a meeting time. If two Princesses only know each other through the Dreamlands it can take several nights of back and forth messages through a court functionary to arrange a meeting. The second advantage is precisely that it is open to more than just Princesses. A Princess taking part in a national conversation on the issues on the day will want to reach both mortals and other Nobles; and she might feel more comfortable with debating her peers in public than getting together with other Princesses to debate in secret and decide on the party line. Even if nobody outside the loop knows who's Noble, having the discussion in the open is the important thing.

As a rule the internet is used for bigger impersonal issues and as a second way to relax and socialise outside of Cliques. It helps a Princess quickly and conveniently keep her finger on the pulse of social issues, Noble or otherwise, build her network of contacts and emergency phone numbers, and means that if you suddenly stop posting another Princess or two might notice and check if you're overworked or in trouble and need help, if you're lucky. But when things get serious Princesses move the conversation to the Dreamlands or phone calls (while calls can be traced, the people with the resources to do that usually aren't interested in random youngsters like a Princess' mundane identity). In the Dreamlands a Noble can ask the other Nobles who'll be joining the same university at her if any wish to form a Nakama with a degree of confidence that everyone in the room is at least a Princess. Or she can just discuss Charms and magical techniques without attracting the wrong sort of attention. 

\subsection*{Campaigns}

Princesses may disagree with each other about how to improve the world, and even about what a better world would even look like, but in the face of an emergency they are quick to put their philosophising and debates aside and unite behind a Campaign. Whole Nakamas, Cliques, and independent Princesses travel from all over the land to pit their combined strength against a problem. 

A Campaign always starts when Princesses face a large immediate problem. In a long career a Princess might see Campaigns against large Darkspawn incursions or to rebuild after natural disasters. More complicated problems where every political ideology has it's own mutually opposing solution may draw campaigns of like minded Princesses, sometimes this require negotiations to prevent two Campaigns arriving in the same town and working at cross purposes and sometimes the negotiations fail and two campaigns do end up competing for resources and a political mandate. Most campaigns though, form around large attention grabbing problems. Princesses have a lot of responsibilities on their plate, it can take something big to call them away from home. 

The concept of a Campaign is well known to the Noble community, and most begin deliberately. Princesses announce that there will be a meeting in a Queen's palace (usually the Queen of Hearts) at a certain time and see if enough people turn up. It is generally considered a bad sign if only one Noble starts trying to arrange a Campaign, if a problem doesn't convince several Nobles they need to start a campaign it is unlikely to convince a large group of Nobles they should join a campaign. Of course, this rule of thumb depends on the size of the Princess population at ground zero. 

A Queen's palace not chosen just because it's a convenient meeting point, the gathered Radiant often ask the Queen to select a Campaign's leader and officers. Even Princesses with an ideological commitment to democracy, and there are a lot of them, usually agree that if a Queen can't pick a leader who's acceptable to everyone then this issue isn't the sort of direct problem suitable for a campaign and it would be better for Nobles to work on solving the issue in their own way than spend their time arguing about how to solve the issue. 

A Campaign doesn't always formally end when it achieves it's aim. After working or fighting with fellow gregarious Nobility everyone is sure to have made friends and that provides a powerful incentive to keep the campaign running and give everyone a reason to stay away from their responsibilities back home a little longer. When the campaign succumbs to this temptation the leader begin expanding the Campaign's scope and a ideological commitments, after the Darkspawn are chased out the leader proposes the Campaign beings fighting for political reforms to prevent new Taint forming. Once this happens Princesses who disagree with the new direction quickly depart, usually the Court of Spades leave first and serve as the proverbial canary in the coal mine. This has happened often enough that it's become a running joke among the Radiant and Campaigns that do formally disband after achieving their aims often announce their end with a bombastic speech announcing a radical expansion of their mission, followed by a good natured prank from the Spades. 

It's not unusual for a completed Campaign to leave behind a few Nakamas who do continue it's original mission with an expanded scope. And many Princesses who arrived alone go home as part of a Nakama or Clique, but it's considered a very worrying sign when a Princess insists she is still the leader after everyone's departed or tries to prevent people from leaving.

\subsection*{Language}

The Hopeful are for the most part a young culture and even the parts of it that regularly communicate don't have any formal body to standardise language. While there is a linguistic legacy that pre-dates the Release, three in fact: One is in the Royal Tongue (which many Princesses find cumbersome, or just annoying) another is in Alhambran (which many Radiant refuse to use on principle), the third comes from the Embassy to the Dreamlands (which is specialised and relatively obscure). Consequently the Radiant use a fast changing system of slang, memes and technical terms that vary across geographical regions and even parts of the internet. The language of the Radiant is young enough that most words can easily be traced back to their origin. The following terms are usually used by Princesses in the anglosphere, having been spread across that portion of the internet. Some of the more specific terms are unique to American Princesses. 

The tradition of naming Queens after playing cards came early after Princesses searched for an alternative to the Queen's titles in the Royal Tongue. The Queen of Diamonds suggested \textquotedblleft The Queen of Diamonds\textquotedblright\, (as well as \textquotedblleft The Crystal-eyed One\textquotedblright ) as a two very rough summaries of her most important titles. One of the Princesses present at the time started referring to the other Queens with different playing cards, since none of the other Queens had made English suggestions yet (or been asked yet, this happened quite early) it stuck. There are actually several alternative card based naming schemes, most attempt to try and match the Queens to card based symbolism. Either through their invocation's element or by trying to match their character to a feudal class. Neither are popular, partly because the symbolism doesn't work too well, partly because if the symbolism of playing cards has any useful magical properties it doesn't apply to Noble magic, and 
partly because the Queen of Diamonds prefers to be called the Queen of Diamonds. The common system is found throughout the English speaking world. Different naming schemes can be found elsewhere, but it is more common for each Queen to offer a set of unrelated rough translations into the local language. 

Terms in Japanese originate from Bonnie Getsuei, an American (and complete Japanophile) who forged much of the Radiant's community in San Francisco and even provided a common entry point to the online Radiant community for English speaking princesses (presumably actual Japanese Princesses also use their native tongue). 

Italian words can often trace their origin to the Queen of Heart's earliest followers in the United Kingdom (The Court of Hearts had a late start in America, as the first impressions of that court often clash with America's national ideals. Today few Princesses even remember what the fuss was about.) Influenced by Andartan culture those Princesses chose to use a romance language, and since they didn't want to use French; Princesses everywhere sprinkle their magic with Italian terms. 

Greek and Latin words both share origins in the Court of Diamonds; the early Lights were the first to attempt to begin classifying and studying Noble magic and related phenomena. This project was split between those who were more influenced by their Danann aesthetics and wanted to use Greek and those who were more influenced by human academia and wanted to use Latin. The argument was never settled and various words from both languages have become part of the Princess lexicon, mostly for things a Princess can go a while without encountering as everything else was named through informal, and therefore faster, channels. Exceptionally nerdy Princesses sometimes use Greek instead of the Latin terms or Latin instead of the Greek, and Latin or Greek instead of English, Italian and Japanese terms. 

The Embassy to the Dreamlands, perhaps the only lasting Radiant organisation to exist through the Long Night, tended to use names taken from people both living, Dreamlander and even fictional (these are rarely distinct categories). Few of these terms are still in use. 

Finally English is, obviously, the de-facto language of English speaking Princesses. Anything that did not get a catchy enough name quickly enough is likely to be in English. One unusual, and uniquely American, fact about English is that many Princesses adopt a more \textquotedblleft royal\textquotedblright\, manner of speaking in their Transformed identity, which is to say a British accent ranging from \textquotedblleft BBC English\textquotedblright\ to \textquotedblleft not even The Queen lays it on that thick\textquotedblright. Princesses who are actually from the United Kingdom usually use their natural accent, though as the British will parody anything some have been known to play up, or adopt, incomprehensible regional accents when around Americans.

\subsection*{The Twilight Courts}

Among the Nobility there is no agreed way to help the world. Princesses who advocate for collective solutions spar across the debating stage with Nobles who focus upon individual liberty. In laboratories Princesses push the boundaries of technology while on the other side of the university campus their peers argue for a return to the good old ways. But among the Nobility an informal understanding holds, to Princesses it's so natural and obvious that nobody gave it a name. The understanding states that even when you disagree with a fellow Radiant's idea of a better world you can still respect her as a person, and while her utopia may not be yours it is at least better than today's world under a shroud of Darkness. 

But this understanding only holds to the Radiant. No such benefit of the doubt is given to the Princesses of the Twilight Courts. 

The Twilight Courts are thrice separated from the Radiant. They are separated by politics, the Radiant and Twilight courts will at best clench their teeth and swallow their distaste before teaming up against a mutual foe and at worst violently oppose each other. They are separated by magic, for the five Radiant Queens are neighbours in the Dreamlands and their Invocations demand nothing more than virtuous behaviour as each Queen defines it. The three Twilight Queens each exist alone and their Invocations take a toll upon the user's mind, body, or soul. Finally they are separated by morality, for the philosophies of the Twilight Courts directly harm innocents in a way that the Radiant Courts do not (On this point the Court of Mirrors is more complicated, and so it is sometimes held to be on the Radiant side). Thus even the most inexperienced Princess cannot help but see that there is a division between Radiant and Twilight on the deepest levels.

And though the five Radiant Queens and their Courts stand together. No such camaraderie exists amongst the Twilight Courts: The Courts of Tears and Storms have been in open war for perhaps as long as they have existed. And the harsh dogmatic outlook of those two Courts means that they are less tolerant of the Court of Mirrors than Radiant Princesses. Each Twilight Court stands in isolation or with the Radiant. 

But perhaps the deepest reason for conflict between the Radiant and Twilight Courts is that for once even Nobles want to remain in conflict. Alone or among their trusted friends the Radiant admit that a part of them is tempted by the philosophies of the Twilight Courts. What Princess hasn't put her beloved sister's safety above a stranger's? Felt hatred for the Darkness' evil? Clung a little too hard to a reassuring Belief or assumed a fellow Radiant is good and virtuous when they first met just because she's Noble? And when the Radiant reject the part of them that's tempted to walk down that long road paved with good intentions they also reject their kinship to every Princess who's walked that road to the very end. 

For all their light, Princesses are still human and think in very human ways.

Princesses are sent to bring Light to the world. Every kind word she speaks, every inspiring song she sings, makes the world just that bit brighter. But a Princess' mission will not be easy, and not every obstacle she faces is born of ignorance, misunderstandings, or legitimate differences of opinion. Just as Princesses are sent to deliver Light unto the world the Outer Darkness also has it's servants, sent forth to smother the land in misery and despair. 

In the centuries where the Darkness stood unopposed by the Light's champions mankind learned to defend itself. Very few parts of the world can call themselves safe and free from the Darkness' predations but in many places the Darkness is forced back into the Shadows by the vigilance and sacrifice of mortal monster hunters, and in modern nights the victories come quicker and at less cost as Princesses pledge their blades to the cause. In these places a Princess who believes her time and magic is best invested elsewhere can have a long and productive career without ever fighting a monster. 

But elsewhere the Darkness rules, humans are little more than prey for monsters stalking the night. Despair fills the air and every day another friend gives up hope and joins the ranks of the desperate, his body warping into a form as monstrous as his behaviour. Any Princess who lives here is sure to become a target if she lets her Light shine, but few Princesses let danger deter them from their duties, for such areas cry out for a saviour and many among the Radiant give up their comfortable surroundings to go where they are most needed. If they do not the Court of Storms are sure to do so; for no matter how deeply rooted the Darkness is, fire can always burn it out, along with anyone unlucky enough to be nearby. 



The Darkness comes to every place where one person makes another suffer, and where people sink into despair. At sites of enormous cruelty - where depraved scientists performed dreadful experiments on human subjects; where kidnapped girls were sold into sexual slavery; where hardened killers waited to ambush their victims - the call of pain and fear brings it in crashing like a wave of foul water. Lesser evils, if constantly repeated \textemdash\, offices whose managers wield their authority to reduce everyone to quivering bootlickers; streets haunted by muggers who strip passersby of valuables; houses of drunkards and addicts who think of nothing past the next fix; quiet suburbs full of backbiters, talebearers and petty rivalries \textemdash\, let it drift in and build up slowly, like thickening smog.
